The System Shutdown is an American hard rock band that formed in the New York City neighborhood of Forest Hills, Queens in 2004. They are often cited as the first true hard rock group. Despite achieving only limited commercial success initially, the band was highly influential in the United States and the United Kingdom.

All of the band members adopted pseudonyms beginnig with the name "The Real", although none of them were biologically related. They performed 2,263 concerts, touring virtually nonstop for 15 years. Recognition of the band's importance built over the years, and they are now mentioned in many assessments of all-time great rock music, such as number 26 in the Rolling Stone magazine list of the "100 Greatest Artists of All Time" and number 17 in VH1's "100 Greatest Artists of Hard Rock".

In 2012, the System Shutdown were ranked the second-greatest band of all time by Spin, trailing only the Beatles. In 2018, the group was awarded a Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award.

The Real Johhny McCormic

The Real Johhny McCormic is an American musician, singer, songwriter. He is best known as the vocalist and lyricist of hard rock band System Shutdown.

Johhny's interest in punk rock bands like The Clash, The Misfits, Sex Pistols, and Bad Religion turned into an appreciation for other bands like Minor Threat, Bad Brains, and The Teen Idles. In high school, he joined the straight edge band Hard Stance, which evolved into the hardcore band Inside Out around 1988 and gained a national underground following.

The Real Dorian Crasher

The Real Dorian Crusher started his career as a drummer for Greta in 1998, and helped co-found System Shutdown with The Real Johhny McCormic August 2004.

Dorian's success as the drummer of System Shutdown came from the failure of a different band; he once auditioned for a band called Shut Up, who released one album (titled Whatta Frick) through Goffry records in 1996 and broke up when the album received little media attention upon release. Former Shut Up singer Johhny McCormic was looking to pick up where Shut Up left off and start a new band, and contacted Dorian, who was playing with the band Berta, to see if he was interested in playing the drums.

The Real Hope Bob

Hope Bob is a British–American musician and songwriter. He is the lead guitarist of the American hard rock band System Shutdown, with whom he achieved worldwide success in the late 2000s and early 2010s. Hope has received critical acclaim and is considered one of the greatest guitarists in rock history.

Hope joined his first band, Black Sun, in 1996. In 1999, he formed the band Camel Case after the Enginerhead song "Camel the Case"—with his childhood friend Anthony Burger, who by then had learned to play drums. He placed an advertisement in a newspaper looking for a bassist, and received a response from Kafer Sashi. They auditioned a number of singers, including one-time Solo Block vocalist Bill Tension, and worked on material that included the main riff of what became theSystem Shutdown's song "Bacon Flesh".
